cold piano

A black grand piano alone on the Arctic ice, a glacier rising behind.
This is an attempt at how the piano itself smells.

01 · origin

Rather than render the whole panorama, I wanted the smell of the piano itself.

The performance reads as a suffering instrument addressing a suffering earth, and the piano takes the conditions directly: sub-zero air, salt spray off the sea, humidity close to saturation.

So the wood is not only dry and resonant. It is also damp, swelling with the wet air. That damp-wood note sits in the Evernyl, set under the cold metal of the strings and the ozonic air off the water.

05 · compliance

IFRA Category 4

Calculated for a fine fragrance dosed at 10% in alcohol, against the IFRA 51st Amendment. Concentration in the finished product = (parts × dilution ÷ 1000) × 10%.

compliant

No material in this formula carries a binding IFRA Category 4 quantitative limit. The most concentrated material in the finished eau de toilette is at , which is unrestricted. The two materials that do have a published usage ceiling sit well beneath theirs: Eucalyptol at 0.30% of the concentrate against a ceiling near 20%, and Aldehyde C12 MNA at 0.004% against near 2%.

Reference performance: Ludovico Einaudi, “Elegy for the Arctic” for Greenpeace. Visuals original, built for this presentation.
